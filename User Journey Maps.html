User Journey Maps — A Week in the Life

Two perspectives mapped across the same week. The Wednesday exchange appears in both maps — it is the same moment seen from each side.

Persona 1
The Away Person
25, studying abroad. Family in another country. Feels guilty for not calling more.

Scenario

A typical week. The family chat has been quiet — mostly logistics for several days. The away person wants to connect but doesn't know how to start something real. Both features come into play across the week.

What
they do

Checks family chat. Sees nothing new. Types "hope you're well" — deletes it. Puts phone down.

Opens app during commute. Sees a prompt card: "Your mum mentioned she started a new cooking class."

Sends a voice note asking about the class. Gets a long reply — first real exchange in 10 days.

Takes a photo of dinner, thinks of family. Shares it to the family chat with a short message.

App flags the voice note exchange. Sees: "Save this to your vault?" Confirms.

Receives weekly recap card. Sees the week summarised. Tags one more moment manually.

What
they feel

Guilty. Wants to reach out but the blank chat feels like a barrier with no starting point.

Surprised. The prompt gives something specific to respond to — pressure to "start something" is gone.

Relieved and connected. The conversation felt natural, not forced or obligatory.

A small moment of closeness — sharing from daily life without needing a reason to call.

Good that the moment won't just scroll away. A little proud of it.

Satisfied. The week had more connection than it felt like in the moment.

Persona 2
The Family at Home
63, parent. Child lives abroad. Feels out of the loop. Doesn't always know how to reach across the distance.

Scenario

The same week, from the other side. The parent checks in passively and waits. The service gives them a richer sense of what's going on in their child's life — without requiring new behaviour from either side.

What
they do

Sends a morning emoji to the group chat. Checks if child has been active. Nothing new. Puts phone down.

Opens the app. Sees a quiet digest of what the child has been sharing recently — no call needed to feel up to date.

Child sends a voice note about the cooking class. Parent sends a long reply — first real exchange in 10 days.

Receives a photo of child's dinner. Replies with a heart and a short message.

Gets a notification: "A moment from this week has been saved to your vault." Opens it. Listens again.

Receives weekly recap. Also sees: "One year ago, [child] shared their first week abroad." Sends it back to the chat.

What
they feel

Passive. Waiting. Drops something in the chat to signal presence but doesn't know what's happening in the child's life.

More informed than usual. Has something real to respond to — not just "how are you?"

Warm and present. The conversation felt like it came naturally rather than out of obligation.

Included. The small photo makes the distance feel smaller — a glimpse into daily life.

The moment has been caught before it could scroll away. Feels like something worth keeping.

Nostalgic and newly connected. The year-ago memory sparks a real moment right now.
